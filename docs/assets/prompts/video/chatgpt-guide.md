1. Global Visual Grammar (use in EVERY scene)

Copy this block into every prompt:

[Cinematography]: Medium shot, static camera, slow gentle pacing. Use subtle pauses (1–2 seconds) at key learning moments. Framing is centered and balanced for maximum readability.

[Characters]: Consistent hand-drawn line-art characters: one toddler and one parent. Soft rounded outlines, minimal facial features but clear emotions. Maintain identical appearance across all scenes.

[Style]: Soft educational 2D animation. Blend of whiteboard and children's book illustration. Warm pastel palette (cream, peach, soft green, gentle blue). Clean, uncluttered backgrounds.

[Lighting]: Flat, even, bright lighting. No shadows or dramatic contrast. Prioritize clarity over realism.

[Educational Overlay Rules]:

- Always include 1 main concept label (top of frame)
- Use short, simple phrases (max 6–8 words)
- Use consistent font and placement
- Reinforce meaning visually, not just text

[Timing Rules]:

- Hold key moments for 1–2 seconds:
  1. Problem (frustration / confusion)
  2. Communication attempt
  3. Parent response
  4. Positive outcome

This is your ready-to-use prompt:

[Cinematography]: Medium shot, static camera, slow gentle pacing. Use subtle pauses (1–2 seconds) at key learning moments. Framing is centered and balanced for maximum readability.

[Characters]: Consistent hand-drawn line-art characters: one toddler and one parent. Soft rounded outlines, minimal facial features but clear emotions.

[Action Sequence]:

1. The toddler stands in a simple kitchen and reaches toward a cookie jar placed on a high shelf. The toddler shows mild frustration (cannot reach).
   (Hold 1–2 seconds)

2. The toddler turns to the parent and makes eye contact.
   (Brief pause)

3. A small, clear speech bubble appears: "cookie!"
   (Hold 1–2 seconds, subtly emphasize this moment)

4. The parent immediately understands, smiles warmly, reaches for the cookie jar, and gives the cookie to the toddler.

5. The toddler becomes visibly happy and relaxed.
   (Hold 1–2 seconds)

[Context]: Minimalist kitchen. Only essential elements visible (shelf + jar). No clutter.

[Style & Ambiance]: Soft educational 2D animation. Warm pastel palette (cream, peach, soft green, gentle blue). Clean whiteboard + children's book style.

[Lighting]: Flat, bright, even lighting.

[Educational Overlays]:
Top of frame:
"Requesting = asking for what your child wants"

During speech moment (step 3), add small label near bubble:
"Child communicates"

At outcome (step 5), briefly show:
"Communication works"

[Purpose]:
Make it immediately clear that the child’s communication leads to getting what they want.
